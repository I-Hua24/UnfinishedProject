Font style

I'm sure we all remember playing around the fonts on the word document and seeing the flashy quirky wordd arts. Fonts are a fun way to express some personality and style. but let's be honest some fonts can make your content look boring even if you are talking about the most exciting topic in the world. and there's no use of posting the content if the font makes it unreadable as well. given that we have a decent amount population that is dyslxic, it's a lost opportunity if we dont consider them as well.

After some research and testing (with ChatGPT, yes, I know I was being a bit lazy), I found that the font "Atkinson Hyperlegible" is an excellent choice for readability and accessibility. However, it is not the most widely available font, so the next most readable font that is also widely available is Arial. Therefore, my website is set to use the following CSS: font-family: "Atkinson Hyperlegible", Arial, "Helvetica", sans-serif; I was also advised that spacing and line height are important for readability, so I have added the following to my CSS as well: line-height: 1.6; letter-spacing: 0.03em; Of course, different content may call for different fonts for professionalism or fun, so it is important to browse and experiment with different fonts to find the one that best suits your content and audience.
